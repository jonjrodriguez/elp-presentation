Why do security and culture clash?

Why do security and culture clash?

  • Security is seen as an inconvenience to many
  • Culture does not support the need for a secure campus
  • Big brother monitoring not favored by the community
  • Free exchange of data expected
  • Convenience of a single, open campus
  • Trust issues

Annoyances of security

  • Password changes and SSO integration
  • Lack of central authority, individual accountability and communication
  • People glaze over
  • Lack of user training / education
  • Lack of accountability / communication

Obstacle for security

  • We own the data but we have no technology background
  • Pressure to develop solutions quickly
  • Expose data through oversight in applications
  • Difficult to manage many operating systems
  • NYU IT teams not closely communicating
  • Vendors not compliant with industry specs
  • Don't learn from previously unsuccessful methods of training the user
  • High expectations

Where do culture clashes and solutions meet?

Community Outreach / Education

  • Locking workstation
  • Personally liable for breaches - not NYU
  • Identifying security vulnerabilities from end user perspective

Common misconceptions

  • Technology providers and systems managers are solely responsible
  • The inconvenience is not worthwhile
  • Users are naturally skeptical

Solutions to culture vs security

  • Users want to know why certain security measures are in place
  • Shared responsibility. Partly the user, partly the organization
  • Listen to the needs of the user
  • Make it easier for them
  • Be careful what you put online
  • Educate the user and ensure they are protected
  • It's our responsibility as IT professionals -- and leaders
